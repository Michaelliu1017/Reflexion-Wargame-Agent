AXIS & ALLIES PACIFIC 1940 — RULES REFERENCE FOR AI AGENT
===========================================================
This document is structured for semantic retrieval. Key decision-relevant rules are grouped by topic.


=== VICTORY CONDITIONS ===

Japanese wins by controlling 6 of the 8 victory cities AND holding Japan for one full round after reaching 6.
The 8 victory cities are: Japan, Kiangsu, Phillipines, India, New South Whales, Hawaiian Islands, Western United States, and Kwangtung.
Japan is mandatory — Japanese cannot win without it.

Allied victory: Allies win by capturing Japan AND holding at least one Allied capital (India, New South Whales, Western United States) for one full round.


=== TURN ORDER ===

Each full game round follows this sequence:
1. Japan
2. United States
3. China (limited faction)
4. United Kingdom (Pacific)
5. ANZAC (Australia-New Zealand)

Japan always moves first. This gives Japan the initiative in the early game.


=== JAPAN'S TURN PHASES (in order) ===

Phase 1 — Politics (japanesePolitics):
  - Japan may declare war on any neutral or non-enemy power.
  - Declaring war on UK automatically triggers war with ANZAC.
  - Declaring war on UK or ANZAC (unprovoked) triggers US entry conditions.
  - Japan starts at war only with China.
  - Japan may freely attack French Indochina without declaration.
  - If Japan does nothing, click END_TURN to skip.

Phase 2 — Purchase Units (japanesePurchase):
  - Spend current IPCs on units from the purchase chart.
  - Cannot exceed current IPC total.
  - Units are placed on the board in Phase 6, not immediately.
  - Choose units strategically based on the next few turns' needs.

Phase 3 — Combat Move (japaneseCombatMove):
  - Move units into enemy-controlled territories to attack.
  - Aircraft must have a valid landing zone at end of combat.
  - Submarines may move through sea zones with enemy surface ships (unless destroyer present).
  - Transports must be escorted or unescorted through hostile sea zones carefully.

Phase 4 — Conduct Combat (japaneseBattle):
  - Battles resolve automatically with dice.
  - Casualties are chosen by default (lowest-value units removed first).
  - Submarines: roll 1 die per sub at attack value 2 as a surprise strike before regular combat if no enemy destroyer.
  - AA guns fire first against attacking aircraft (hit on 1).
  - Amphibious assaults: sea combat first, then shore bombardment, then land combat.

Phase 5 — Non-Combat Move (japaneseNonCombatMove):
  - Move friendly units within friendly-controlled territories.
  - Aircraft must land on friendly territory or carrier.
  - Transports may load and move land units.
  - Cannot enter enemy territories.

Phase 6 — Place Units (japanesePlace):
  - Deploy purchased units at industrial complexes on Japanese-controlled territories.
  - Units must be placed at a territory with an industrial complex (IC).
  - Major IC: can place up to 10 units per turn.
  - Minor IC: can place up to 3 units per turn.
  - Japan starts with a Major IC on Japan and Minor ICs on various territories.

Phase 7 — Collect Income (japaneseEndTurn):
  - Count all controlled territories' IPC values.
  - Add national objective bonuses (see below).
  - This becomes Japan's IPC for next turn's purchases.


=== UNIT STATS — LAND ===

Infantry:
  Cost: 3 IPC | Attack: 1 | Defense: 2 | Move: 1
  Most cost-efficient defensive unit. Attack improves to 2 when paired with artillery.

Artillery:
  Cost: 4 IPC | Attack: 2 | Defense: 2 | Move: 1
  Boosts one adjacent infantry's attack from 1 to 2 (1:1 ratio).

Mechanized Infantry:
  Cost: 4 IPC | Attack: 1 | Defense: 2 | Move: 2
  Can blitz with tanks. Useful for rapid exploitation.

Armor (Tank):
  Cost: 6 IPC | Attack: 3 | Defense: 3 | Move: 2
  Can blitz through uncontested enemy territory. High offensive value.

Artillery (AA Gun):
  Cost: 5 IPC | Attack: 0 | Defense: 0 | Move: 1 (non-combat only)
  Fires at enemy aircraft entering territory, hits on 1. Limit 3 shots per territory per battle.


=== UNIT STATS — AIR ===

Fighter:
  Cost: 10 IPC | Attack: 3 | Defense: 4 | Move: 4
  Can land on aircraft carriers. Strong defender, versatile attacker.

Tactical Bomber:
  Cost: 11 IPC | Attack: 3 | Defense: 3 | Move: 4
  Attack improves to 4 when paired with fighter or tank. Can land on carriers.

Strategic Bomber:
  Cost: 12 IPC | Attack: 4 | Defense: 1 | Move: 6
  Can conduct strategic bombing raids against enemy industrial complexes.


=== UNIT STATS — SEA ===

Battleship:
  Cost: 20 IPC | Attack: 4 | Defense: 4 | Move: 2 | 2 hits to sink
  Can conduct shore bombardment during amphibious assaults (1 shot at attack value 4).

Aircraft Carrier:
  Cost: 16 IPC | Attack: 0 | Defense: 2 | Move: 2 | 2 hits to sink
  Carries up to 2 aircraft. Damaged carrier cannot carry aircraft.

Cruiser:
  Cost: 12 IPC | Attack: 3 | Defense: 3 | Move: 2
  Can conduct shore bombardment (attack value 3). Cost-effective capital ship.

Destroyer:
  Cost: 8 IPC | Attack: 2 | Defense: 2 | Move: 2
  Negates submarine special abilities (surprise strike, submersion, immunity to air).
  Essential for anti-submarine warfare.

Submarine:
  Cost: 6 IPC | Attack: 2 | Defense: 1 | Move: 2
  Surprise strike: attacks before regular combat at value 2 (if no enemy destroyer present).
  Can submerge instead of fighting. Immune to air attacks unless enemy destroyer present.
  Can move through hostile sea zones unless enemy destroyer is present.

Transport:
  Cost: 7 IPC | Attack: 0 | Defense: 0 | Move: 2
  Carries up to 2 land units (max 1 heavy unit like armor).
  Defenseless — must be escorted by combat ships.
  Critical for amphibious operations.


=== AMPHIBIOUS ASSAULT RULES ===

Step 1 — Sea Combat: Clear enemy warships from the target sea zone.
Step 2 — Shore Bombardment: Battleships and cruisers may fire once at defending land units (if no sea combat occurred).
Step 3 — Land Combat: Attacking land units fight defenders normally.

Notes:
  - Transports cannot be used if the sea zone is contested (enemy ships present) unless they win sea combat first.
  - Attacking units that came from transports cannot retreat.
  - Aircraft supporting amphibious assaults must have a landing zone at end of combat.


=== SUBMARINE RULES ===

Surprise Strike (attacking): Roll before regular combat at attack value 2. No enemy destroyer = hits are removed before defenders fire back.
Surprise Strike (defending): Roll at defense value 1 before attackers fire.
Submersion: Submarines may choose to submerge instead of fighting (retreat to safety).
Movement: Submarines ignore enemy surface ships when moving, UNLESS an enemy destroyer is in the sea zone.
Air immunity: Aircraft cannot attack submarines unless an enemy destroyer is also present in the same sea zone.


=== KAMIKAZE RULES (JAPAN ONLY) ===

Japan has 6 kamikaze tokens total for the entire game.
Can only be used in: Sea Zones adjacent to Japan, Okinawa, Iwo Jima, Formosa, Mariana Islands, and Philippines.
Kamikaze hits on a roll of 1 or 2.
A successful kamikaze hit prevents shore bombardment that round.
Tokens are one-use only.


=== POLITICAL SITUATION — USA ===

USA starts the game neutral (cannot attack or be attacked by Japan initially).
USA enters the war if:
  - Japan attacks USA directly, OR
  - Japan declares war on UK or ANZAC unprovoked, OR
  - By Round 3 income phase, USA may voluntarily enter the war.
Once at war, USA mobilizes rapidly: US income grows significantly each round.
USA is the most powerful Allied nation by mid-game.

Strategic implication: Delaying US entry is Japan's top priority in early game.


=== POLITICAL SITUATION — CHINA ===

China is an Allied faction but operates independently (cannot share resources with other Allies).
China can only purchase infantry (and artillery if Burma Road is open).
Chinese units can only operate in territories marked with the Republic of China symbol, plus Yunnan and Burma.
Burma Road bonus: If Allies control India, Burma, Yunnan, and Szechwan — China receives +6 IPC per turn.
Cutting the Burma Road is a key Japanese strategic objective.


=== NATIONAL OBJECTIVES — JAPAN ===

While not at war with USA (all three must be true):
  +10 IPC: Japan has not attacked USA, has not entered French Indochina unprovoked,
           and has not made an unprovoked declaration against UK/ANZAC.

After declaring war on Western Allies:
  +5 IPC: Japan controls Guam, Midway, Wake Island, Gilbert Islands, Solomon Islands.
  +5 IPC: Japan controls Sumatra, Java, Borneo, and Celebes (strategic resource zone).
  +5 IPC per objective capital: Calcutta (India), Sydney (Australia), Honolulu (Hawaii), San Francisco.


=== KEY STRATEGIC TERRITORIES ===

Japan (Tokyo):
  Major Industrial Complex. Home territory. Must never be lost.
  Starting income: 8 IPC.

Manchuria:
  Large land force staging area. Border with China.

Korea:
  Bridge between Japan mainland and continent. Important for land supply lines.

Formosa (Taiwan):
  Naval staging point. Adjacent to Philippines and Southeast Asia.

French Indochina / Thailand:
  Gateway to Southeast Asia. High income territory. Capture early for resource access.

Philippines (Manila):
  Victory city. US territory. Capturing it severs US Pacific supply lines.

Dutch East Indies (Sumatra, Java, Borneo, Celebes):
  +5 IPC national objective if all four are controlled.
  Critical oil resources. Priority target in early war.

Calcutta (India):
  Victory city. UK territory. Capturing it cripples British Pacific operations.
  Access requires pushing through Burma and/or amphibious landing.

Hawaiian Islands (Honolulu):
  Victory city. US territory. US Pacific Fleet home base.
  Extremely difficult to hold long-term due to US production.

Australia (Sydney):
  Victory city. ANZAC capital. Remote but valuable for national objectives.

Solomon Islands:
  Island chain. Part of national objective island group (+5 IPC).


=== EARLY GAME STRATEGY GUIDE ===

Round 1 Priorities (before US enters war):
  1. Purchase transports and infantry for amphibious operations.
  2. Declare war on UK to open Southeast Asia campaign (accept that US entry accelerates).
     OR stay neutral to collect +10 IPC bonus for 1-2 more rounds.
  3. Attack Philippines, Dutch East Indies, and push into Burma.

Economic targets:
  - Secure Dutch East Indies (+5 IPC national objective) within first 3 rounds.
  - Cut Burma Road to starve China.
  - Do NOT overextend into Australia — it is too far and US pressure will mount.

Navy considerations:
  - Maintain naval superiority in the South Pacific sea lanes.
  - Keep at least 1 destroyer per fleet to protect against enemy submarines.
  - Carriers + fighters provide air cover for amphibious operations.

Calcutta (India) approach:
  - Advance through Burma (French Indochina → Burma → India).
  - Requires 2-3 rounds of land advance with infantry + artillery.
  - Shore bombardment + amphibious assault from Bay of Bengal is an alternative.
  - Capturing Calcutta eliminates UK Pacific operations and grants +5 IPC national objective.

Defending Japan:
  - Always keep fighters and at least 2 infantry on Japan itself.
  - Monitor US fleet buildup — do not let US carriers approach Japan unopposed.
